The Power of Order Blocks: A Deep Dive

By Crypto Analyst | Published on July 5, 2024

In the dynamic world of cryptocurrency trading, identifying key supply and demand zones is paramount. Order blocks stand out as a powerful concept derived from Smart Money Concepts (SMC) that can provide traders with an edge by revealing where institutional money has likely entered or exited the market.

At its core, an order block is the last opposing candle before a strong impulsive move that breaks market structure. This move often leaves behind an "imbalance" or "Fair Value Gap" (FVG), signaling an area where price is likely to return to rebalance before continuing its original direction.

What Makes an Order Block Valid?

Not every candle before an impulsive move is an order block. Several criteria enhance its validity:

  • **Break of Structure (BOS):** The impulsive move must break a significant high (for bullish OB) or low (for bearish OB).
  • **Fair Value Gap (FVG):** An inefficiency in price delivery, visible as a gap between the wicks of the first and third candles in a three-candle sequence.
  • **Unmitigated:** The order block zone should not have been revisited and traded through by subsequent price action.
  • **Volume Confluence:** Often, the order block candle itself, or the impulsive candle, will exhibit higher than average volume.

Bullish vs. Bearish Order Blocks

Understanding the direction is crucial:

Bullish Order Blocks (Buying OB)

A bullish order block is typically the last bearish candle before an aggressive bullish move that breaks market structure. Price is expected to return to this zone, which acts as a demand area, before continuing its upward trend.

Bearish Order Blocks (Selling OB)

Conversely, a bearish order block is the last bullish candle before a sharp bearish move that breaks market structure. This zone acts as a supply area where price is likely to retrace to before continuing its downward movement.
